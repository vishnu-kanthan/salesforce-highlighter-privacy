Privacy Policy

Salesforce Case Highlighter Chrome Extension

Last Updated: August 20, 2025

Introduction

This privacy policy outlines the data practices for the "Salesforce Case Highlighter" Chrome Extension. We are committed to protecting your privacy. This extension is designed to function entirely within your browser without collecting or transmitting any of your personal data.

Data We Do Not Collect

We do not collect, store, or transmit any personally identifiable information (PII). This includes, but is not limited to:

  • Your name, email address, or user credentials
  • Your browsing history or website data
  • Any data from your Salesforce account, cases, or customer information
  • Any information about the pages you visit
  • Usage analytics or tracking data

Data We Use

The extension's functionality relies on two types of data that are stored locally on your computer or synced to your own Google account:

User-Configured Settings: To provide a customized experience, the extension saves your preferred highlighting rules and color choices (e.g., breach hour thresholds, highlight colors, configured list view URL). These settings are saved using the chrome.storage.sync API, which syncs them to your personal Google account so your settings are consistent across your browsers. We, the developers, have no access to this data.

Page Content Interaction: The extension reads the content of your active Salesforce Case list view tab to identify case information (priority, status, breach times, etc.) and apply visual highlighting according to your configured rules. This process is done entirely within your browser, and no information about the page content is ever saved, stored, or transmitted outside your browser.

Permissions

The extension requires the following minimal permissions to function:

storage

To save your custom highlighting rules and preferences locally

activeTab

To read and highlight case information on the current Salesforce page when you click the extension

scripting

To inject highlighting functionality into Salesforce pages and apply visual modifications

tabs

To manage extension functionality across Salesforce tabs and reload tabs after configuration changes

notifications

To show helpful setup reminders and welcome messages (optional, can be disabled in browser settings)

Host Permissions

The extension requires access to Salesforce domains (*.salesforce.com, *.force.com, *.lightning.force.com) to provide highlighting functionality on your Salesforce Case list views. This access is used only to enhance the visual display of your existing Salesforce data and does not collect or transmit any information.

Data Security

All extension functionality occurs locally in your browser. No data is sent to external servers, third parties, or our servers. Your Salesforce data remains within your browser and Salesforce's secure environment.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy to reflect changes in functionality or legal requirements. Any changes will be reflected in updated extension versions distributed through the Chrome Web Store, and the "Last Updated" date will be modified accordingly.

Contact Us

If you have any questions about this privacy policy or the extension's data practices, please contact us through the support section of the extension's Chrome Web Store page.
